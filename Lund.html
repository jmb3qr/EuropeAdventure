We had lectures all morning and then we broke for lunch. During lunch a few of us toured the main cathedral in the town square. They are not positive when this cathedral was constructed but Bishop Asser Thorkilsson became the first archbishop for all of Scandinavia in 1104 and the cathedral was begun sometime after he took office. The building was constructed in the typical basilica style with half-rounded arches supporting a flat timber ceiling, and made of sandstone. Lund became the religious heart of Denmark and over the years many monasteries, nunneries, priories sprang up around the cathedral.

After we were done with our presentations we all grabbed lunch and returned our bike to the municipality building. We then got on a train and went to a castle! The castle is called Bosjökloster and is located a little bit north of Lund. This castle was built in 1080 and was originally a nunnery, in the 16th century it was transformed to a castle by the Danish government. It was then donated to the former arch bishop of Lund under the condition that he would take care of the nuns. In 1560 the Danish government was trying to consolidate all of the land they owned to the center of Denmark. In order to do this they made trades with people, people who owned land in the center would trade their land for Danish owned land outside of the city. A women become owner of this castle through one of these trades. Currently this castle is owned and shared by a couple of families.

The palace is open to the public for free and is home to an art gallery/museum and a park. The art museum had a lot of very pretty paintings and sculptures. They have a bunch of farm animals that are all extremely friendly so visitors can play with the animals. They had alpacas, goats, horses, pigs, and chickens. These pictures speak for how much fun we had with these animals. In addition to animals there are a lot of different gardens on the estate and a playground for children. This estate is on a lake and they provide row boats for visitors to take out.
