June 3rd

This morning we got a very early start and took a bus into town and bought an Oslo pass. The Oslo pass is 20 dollars a day for unlimited transportation and admission to any museum in Oslo. The first place we went to was the Opera house, which is very modern and located on the water.

Next we walked to The Akershus which was a castle/fortress that was built to look over the harbor in the late 1290s.

This castle was very important because whoever controlled the castle controlled Norway. The fortress survived countless attacks mostly from Sweden but was never successfully taken over. In 1940 Norway surrendered the fortress to Nazi Germany and on May 11th 1945 Akershus was freed. We went in a museum in the fortress that showcased different kings that lived in the fortress. Inside the fortress there is a chapel and it’s where Kaja was christened.

After the fortress we took the ferry over to the Bygdøy, which is a very rich part of Oslo. We walked to the Norwegian Folk Museum which tries to simulate a walk through the past of Norway. The museum has picked up old buildings from all over Norway and moved them to one park. This allows you to walk through the park and experience what it would be like to travel to all the different regions in Norway. One of the coolest buildings was one of the mid-evil wooden churches called a stave church. There were 2,000 of these churches built across Norway and only 28 of them are still standing.

Next we went to the Viking museum which had Viking boats that dated back to 890. The Vikings sailed all over the world and conquered different areas. They were able to take over these places because their boats were very shallow so they could get all the way into port. It is also said that when the Vikings went into cities they stole all of the pretty girls and took them back to Norway.

After the Viking museum we went to the Kon-Tiki museum which tells the story of Thor Heyerdahl. When Thor was 22 he traveled Marquesas Islands to study zoology and biology. While there he noticed all of the similarities between there and South America. At this time it was believed that the Marquesas Islands were settled by Europeans. He made it is life goal to prove this theory wrong. There is a myth about the Incan sun god, Kon-Tiki, that states that he sailed off into the sea on a raft made of balsa trees and was never heard from again. Thor built a raft of balsa trees and proved that you could sail from South America to Tuamotu Islands. He took 5 other people with him and it took them 101 days to complete the journey.

After he proved this wrong he started doing archeology in Easter Islands. He then built a ship out of reeds and sailed from morocco to Barbados. In all of these adventures he was told his journey was impossible and he proved science and history wrong.

Next we traveled to the Vigeland Sculpture Park in Oslo. This is a giant green space in Oslo, and in the middle of the park is a lot of sculptures of naked people. We had a picnic of wine, crackers, and brie in the park and then went to enjoy the statues.

These sculptures were all designed by Vigeland and are supposed to tell a story about humanity. We all kind of thought the sculptures were really weird and took pictures with them in funny ways.

We spent 12 hours in Oslo being tourist and at the end of the day were exhausted.

The craziest thing here is that they DON'T PAY for sports! Their government pays for everything from the facilities, to the refs, to the uniforms and all of the coaches are volunteer....Kaja and Emilie live in Akershus and within that providence there are 2 big clubs called Nittedal and Gjelleroasen and these clubs have the main sports like soccer, floor ball, swimming, gymnastics, and cross country skiing. For the main sports everything is payed for! In America our parents have to pay thousands and thousands of dollars for us to compleat.
